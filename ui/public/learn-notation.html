How to read Rubik's Cube notation

Have you ever seen something like this: R U R' F' R U R' U' R' F R2 U' R' U'?

This is called Rubik's Cube notation. It is a way to write down the moves of a Rubik's Cube algorithm.

This guide will teach you how to read and write Rubik's Cube notation. We provide 3D animations of the Rubik's Cube algorithms to help you learn.

U

Up face clockwise

U'

Up face counterclockwise. Pronounced 'U prime'

D

Down face clockwise

D'

Down face counterclockwise. Pronounced 'D prime'

R

Right face clockwise

R'

Right face counterclockwise. Pronounced 'R prime'

L

Left face clockwise

L'

Left face counterclockwise. Pronounced 'L prime'

F

Front face clockwise

F'

Front face counterclockwise. Pronounced 'F prime'

B

Back face clockwise

B'

Back face counterclockwise. Pronounced 'B prime'

Double turns

Double turns are followed by a 2. This algorithm is U2 D2 R2 L2 F2 B2

u

Similar to U, but u is a wide turn. Sometimes written as Uw

d

Similar to D, but d is a wide turn. Sometimes written as Dw

r

Similar to R, but r is a wide turn. Sometimes written as Rw

l

Similar to L, but l is a wide turn. Sometimes written as Lw

f

Similar to F, but f is a wide turn. Sometimes written as Fw

b

Similar to B, but b is a wide turn. Sometimes written as Bw

M

Middle slice

M'

Middle slice. Pronounced 'M prime'

E'

Equatorial slice

E'

Equatorial slice. Pronounced 'E prime'

S

Standing slice

S'

Standing slice. Pronounced 'S prime'

x

Cube rotate along x axis

x'

Cube rotate along x axis. Pronounced 'x prime'

y

Cube rotate along y axis

y'

Cube rotate along y axis. Pronounced 'y prime'

z

Cube rotate along z axis

z'

Cube rotate along z axis. Pronounced 'z prime'

Putting it together

As a final test, let's look again at the example from the beginning.

R U R' F' R U R' U' R' F R2 U' R' U'

That is the end of the guide! Now you know how to read and write Rubik's Cube notation.
